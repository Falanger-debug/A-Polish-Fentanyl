Pro-Impotence 

Alcohol and potency: effects of consumption and abstinence

New research sheds light on the links between alcohol consumption, including beer, and potency. The findings indicate potential mechanisms leading to erectile dysfunction (ED) and the possibility of partially reversing these effects after stopping drinking.

Beer and phytoestrogen content

Hops flowers, used as a beer ingredient, contain a strong phytoestrogen - 8-prenylnaringenin. Studies have shown that it has greater estrogenic activity than other known plant estrogens. The 'estrogenic' effect of this substance may influence hormonal balance in men, potentially affecting reproductive functions. However, it should be noted that the concentration of this compound in beer is low, and with moderate consumption, it does not pose a threat.

Abstinence from alcohol improves potency

Studies on individuals with alcohol use disorders showed that up to 88.5% of patients experienced improvement in erectile dysfunction after three months of abstinence. This effect was particularly evident in younger individuals with a shorter history of drinking and no liver damage. These results highlight how destructive chronic drinking is to the cardiovascular system and sexual functions and how essential complete abstinence is in the treatment process.

A meta-analysis indicates a nonlinear relationship between the amount of alcohol consumed and the risk of erectile dysfunction. Moderate drinking may slightly reduce the risk of ED compared to abstinence, likely due to relaxing effects and reduced tension. However, excessive consumption leads to permanent damage to blood vessels, contributing to erectile problems.

Key takeaways

Excessive alcohol consumption:
 Clearly increases the risk of erectile dysfunction, particularly in the case of chronic drinking.

Abstinence:
 Significantly improves sexual functions, as confirmed by clinical studies.

Beer and phytoestrogens:
 While hops contain compounds that may influence hormonal balance, their concentrations in beer, with moderate consumption, should not cause significant effects.
