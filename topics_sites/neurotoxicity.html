Description

Carcinogenicity refers to the ability or potential of a substance, agent, or exposure to cause cancer. A substance that has this property is called a carcinogen. Carcinogens can lead to cancer by causing damage to the DNA in cells or by disrupting cellular metabolic processes, which can lead to uncontrolled cell growth and tumor formation.
